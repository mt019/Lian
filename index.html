Biography

Zhen-Yu (Peter) Lian is a research assistant who focuses on developing nonparametric and semiparametric methods for different statistical problems and data structures especially casual inference and dimension reduction on survival outcomes and machine learning techniques in the Institute of Statistical Science, Academia Sinica.

Peter received his Master’s degree in Statistics at the Department of Mathematics, National Taiwan University (NTU) under the supervision of Professor Shi-Hui Chang. Peter proposed the association analysis of recurrent bivariate event data that may be affected by patient’s characteristics, such as gender, genome type, and other related factors and introduced a new estimated criterion in his master thesis which had been published on the journal of lifetime data analysis.

He graduated from NTU with a bachelor’s degree in Publish Health which enabled him to well-understand the empirical problem of biomedical survival data which motivates him to develop statistical techniques to solve the problem.
